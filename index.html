Sunrise: A Procedurally Generated VR Experience

Sunrise is a single-player, first-person VR exploration experience set in an infinitely expanding urban maze inspired by the dense, winding streets of Hong Kong and Kowloon Walled City.

The environment is built from pre-generated modular assets such as floor tiles and wall segments, which are procedurally assembled at the floor and city-block level as the player explores. Horizontal expansion generates new streets and corridors, while vertical systems introduce stacked apartments, bridges, stairwells, and rooftops to create dense, multi-level navigation. To enable infinite exploration within a limited physical playspace, Sunrise uses room blindness‚Äìbased spatial redirection and forward-only speed manipulation, allowing seamless traversal while maintaining comfort and orientation.

  • Procedural Generation (Hybrid XY / Z Plane Systems)
  • Spatial Redirection & Room Blindness
  • Blender Geometry Node Systems
  • Dynamic Asset, Decal, & Prefab Generation
  • Physics-Based VR Interaction (Climbing / Ladders)

Posematic ‚Äî Motion Capture Data Loader

Posematic is an AI model that converts 2D sketches into 3D models. I developed a PyTorch data loader to convert motion capture datasets (e.g., CMU Mocap, Age Kinematics) into structured tensors for Posematic's training pipeline. The loader parsed skeletal hierarchies, extracted and normalized frame data, and efficiently batched motion sequences for model input to enable realistic motion learning from raw 3D data.

  • PyTorch and Tensors
  • Data Parsing and Preprocessing
  • 3D Kinematics
  • Evaluating Datasets
